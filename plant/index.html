Step by step

Timing

April : Prepare a seedbed or small pots with well-draining soil. Sprinkle basil seeds evenly over the surface, gently press them into the soil, and water lightly. Keep the soil consistently moist.

Soil

Type of soil: Loamy soil: Loamy soil is a mixture of sand, silt, and clay, providing a good balance of drainage, water retention, and nutrients. It is well-aerated and easy to work with.

Soil pH: 6.0-7.5

Planting

Sowing: Indoor Sowing : Sow basil seeds indoors 6-8 weeks before the last expected frost. This involves planting seeds in small containers filled with seed-starting mix, providing adequate light, and keeping the soil moist until seedlings are ready to transplant outdoors.

Depth: 1/4 inch

Germination: Epigeal: The cotyledons (seed leaves) emerge above the ground during germination.

Care

Pests: Aphids, Spider mites, Slugs

Pesticides: Insecticidal soap, Pyrethrin
