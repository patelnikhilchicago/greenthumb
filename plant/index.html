Lettuce romaine calm!

Lettuce is a leafy green vegetable often used in salads. It's relatively easy to grow and comes in various types, like romaine, butterhead, and crisphead. It thrives in cool weather and well-drained soil.

Step by step

Timing

April in temperate locations. : Prepare a garden bed with loose, fertile soil. Sow lettuce seeds directly into the soil in rows, spacing them about an inch apart. Gently cover the seeds with a thin layer of soil and water thoroughly.

Soil

Type of soil: Loamy soil: A balanced mix of sand, silt, and clay that retains moisture well while providing good drainage and aeration. It's rich in nutrients, making it ideal for lettuce growth.

Soil pH: 6.0 to 7.0

Planting

Sowing: Indoor Sowing : Sow the seeds indoors a few weeks before the last expected frost. Plant seeds in trays or pots filled with seed-starting mix. Keep the soil moist and provide adequate light until seedlings are ready to transplant outdoors.

Depth: 1/4 inch

Germination: Epigeal germination: The cotyledons (seed leaves) emerge from the soil as the seedling grows, pulling the developing stem and apical bud with them.

Care

Pests: Aphids, Slugs, Cutworms

Pesticides: Insecticidal soap, Pyrethrin
